The Architecture

From Organisation to Execution
— In Six Layers

ZORBA's structural hierarchy describes how enterprises are organised, with workforce composition defined at every layer — from human-governed corporate structure to agent-dominant delivery.

L1

Organisational Unit

Corporate structure — companies, subsidiaries, divisions, departments. The root of the architecture.

Governance & oversight
L2

Domain

Areas of enterprise activity — Value Chain & Management Functions. The 12 ZORBA reference domains, customisable per industry.

Strategic direction
L3

Capability

What the organisation can do. The critical layer where workforce composition — human, agent, or blended — becomes a design decision.

Blended design
L4

Process

How work flows to deliver capabilities. Where humans and agents most visibly co-exist — orchestrated through defined handoff protocols.

Blended orchestration
L5

Activity

Discrete units of work. The atomic level of workforce composition — each classified by performer type and autonomy level.

Agent-heavy execution
L6

Work

Actual execution — transactions, tasks, outputs. Where architecture meets reality and every instance is traceable back to strategic intent.

Agent-dominant delivery

Strategic Alignment Overlay

Strategy, objectives, and initiatives exist as a cross-cutting overlay — connecting to the structural hierarchy at any level. Every work item can be traced back to strategic intent through both structural and strategic paths.
