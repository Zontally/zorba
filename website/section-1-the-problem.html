The Problem

Enterprise Architecture Was Built for a World That No Longer Exists

The frameworks we've relied on for decades — APQC, TOGAF, COBIT — were designed for organisations staffed entirely by humans. They describe processes, capabilities, and governance as if every participant is a person.

That world is over.

AI agents are now part of the workforce. They execute processes, make decisions within defined boundaries, monitor performance, and collaborate with human colleagues. Yet no reference architecture accounts for this reality. Organisations are building blended workforces on frameworks that don't acknowledge half the workforce exists.

Ad-hoc Automation

Agents deployed without architectural context — no shared understanding of where they fit, what they own, or how they connect to strategy.

Ungoverned Agents

No trust framework, no authority boundaries, no escalation protocols. Agents operating without the governance that human workers take for granted.

No Common Language

Executives, architects, and engineers have no shared vocabulary for describing how humans and AI agents work together across the enterprise.

No Architectural Foundation

The most significant operating model shift in a generation — built on frameworks that were never designed for it.
