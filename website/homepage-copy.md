## SECTION 1: The Problem

**Headline:**
Enterprise Architecture Was Built for a World That No Longer Exists

**Body:**
The frameworks we've relied on for decades — APQC, TOGAF, COBIT — were designed for organisations staffed entirely by humans. They describe processes, capabilities, and governance as if every participant is a person.

That world is over.

AI agents are now part of the workforce. They execute processes, make decisions within defined boundaries, monitor performance, and collaborate with human colleagues. Yet no reference architecture accounts for this reality. Organisations are building blended workforces on frameworks that don't acknowledge half the workforce exists.

The result: ad-hoc automation, ungoverned agents, no shared language for how humans and AI work together, and no architectural foundation for the most significant operating model shift in a generation.

## SECTION 2: What is ZORBA

**Headline:**
A New Foundation for How Business Gets Done

**Body:**
ZORBA — the **Zo**ntally **R**eference **B**usiness **A**rchitecture — is an open framework that defines how enterprises structure themselves from corporate entity to individual unit of work, with AI agents as first-class participants at every level.

The name *Zorba* comes from Greek, meaning "to live each day with passion." Enterprise architecture should be designed with energy and purpose — not filed away and forgotten.

**Three cards / columns:**

**Card 1: Structural Hierarchy**
A six-layer model — Organisational Unit → Domain → Capability → Process → Activity → Work — that describes how any enterprise operates. Flexible enough for a 50-person company or a multinational conglomerate with dozens of business units.

**Card 2: Strategic Alignment**
Strategy and objectives exist as a cross-cutting overlay, connecting to the structural hierarchy at any level. Trace any piece of work back to the strategic intent that initiated it — through both structural and strategic paths.

**Card 3: Blended Workforce**
Every layer defines what humans do, what agents do, how they collaborate, and what governance applies. Workforce composition — the balance of human and agent participation — is an explicit architectural decision, not an afterthought.

## SECTION 3: What Makes ZORBA Different

**Headline:**
Not Another Process Framework with AI Bolted On

**Body / Feature list:**

**Agents Are First-Class Citizens**
ZORBA doesn't treat AI as a technology layer or an automation add-on. Agents are workforce participants with defined roles, autonomy levels, trust profiles, and governance requirements — woven into every layer of the architecture.

**Built for Complex Organisations**
Organisational Units nest recursively — holding companies, subsidiaries, business units, brands, divisions, regions. Each unit can adopt a different industry edition. No forced symmetry. No artificial constraints.

**Value Chain + Management Functions**
Twelve reference domains classified as either Value Chain (industry-specific, differentiating) or Management Functions (industry-agnostic, enabling). Customise your value chain heavily. Adopt management functions closer to the reference.

**Industry Editions**
ZORBA Core is universal. Industry Editions pre-configure the framework for specific verticals — technology service providers, healthcare, financial services, manufacturing, and more. Same underlying model, tailored for your reality.

**Machine-Readable by Design**
The architecture isn't a PDF for a shelf. It's a queryable information model that agents use at runtime to understand their context, authority, and boundaries. Architecture becomes the operating system of the enterprise.

**Framework-Agnostic**
Already using APQC? ITIL? A custom taxonomy? ZORBA's information model supports any classification scheme. Adopt ZORBA's 12 domains, map your existing framework, or run them side by side.

## SECTION 4: The Architecture at a Glance

**Headline:**
From Organisation to Execution — In Six Layers

*(Visual: nested layers diagram)*

| Layer | What It Defines | Workforce Profile |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Organisational Unit** | Corporate structure — companies, divisions, departments | Governance & oversight |
| **Domain** | Areas of enterprise activity — Value Chain & Management Functions | Strategic direction |
| **Capability** | What the organisation can do | Blended design — the key composition decision |
| **Process** | How work flows to deliver capabilities | Blended orchestration — humans and agents co-exist |
| **Activity** | Discrete units of work within processes | Agent-heavy execution |
| **Work** | Actual execution — transactions, tasks, outputs | Agent-dominant delivery |
